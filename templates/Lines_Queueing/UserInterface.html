Your payoff in each round will be given by E + V - (n-1)*C + T.

  • E (Endowment) is the value you own at the beginning of each round.
  • V (Value) is the value of the service you get after you enter the service room.
  • n is your final position in the line in which you are served.
  • C (Cost) is the waiting cost. Whenever someone enters the service room, C will be deducted from your round payoff if you are still in the waiting line. At the beginning of each round, C will be determined randomly for each participant. No one else will know your C and you will not be informed of anyone elseâ€™s C.
  • T (Transfer) is the total transfer you make from exchanging positions with others during the round, which depends on the rule you play.

The upper panel of the user interface shows the parameters and information of current round, including:

  • Round number, Endowment E, service value V, waiting cost C, and current payoff.
  • Exchange rule, whether communication is allowed, and service time (how fast the line moves forward).
  • Remaining time. It is possible that the system has a few seconds lagging based on the quality of your internet connection. As a result, your round may end when the remaining time is still positive.

At the end of each round, you can review your game history from the previous round on your result page.
